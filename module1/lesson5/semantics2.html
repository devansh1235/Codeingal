Why reading is good

Reading books provides significant physical and mental health benefits by improving brain function, vocabulary, focus, memory, and reducing stress, while also increasing knowledge, empathy, and creativity. It serves as both a stimulating and relaxing activity, offers entertainment and companionship, and can even contribute to a longer lifespan.

Side Effects

While books offer many benefits, potential downsides of excessive reading include physical issues like eyestrain, headaches, and a sedentary lifestyle; mental effects such as information overload, cognitive fatigue, and isolation; and practical concerns like the time and money involved in acquiring and storing books
